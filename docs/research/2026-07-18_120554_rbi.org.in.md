# RBI — rbi.org.in (Reserve Bank of India)

_Explored 2026-07-18 (via search + page fetch). RBI issues & manages G-Secs and runs the auctions._

## Why it matters
RBI is the **primary market** for sovereigns — where new G-Secs, T-Bills and SDLs are born (issue
size, auction date, **cut-off yield/price**, bid-cover). That's the one sovereign attribute FBIL/CCIL
don't give (they cover valuation & secondary trading, not issuance). RBI also holds the longest
official macro yield history.

## Surface 1 — Auction press releases (primary issuance)
Index: `https://www.rbi.org.in/scripts/FS_PressRelease.aspx?fn=2757`
- Covers: **GoI Dated Securities** auctions, **T-Bill** auctions (91/182/364-day), **State Govt
  Securities (SGS/SDL)** auctions, **Sovereign Gold Bonds**, underwriting auctions, and
  **auction results + cut-off** data.
- Organized by **year → month, back to 1990**. Each item = announcement date, title, size, **PDF link**
  (`rbidocs.rbi.org.in/rdocs/PressRelease/PDFs/*.PDF`, ~150–510 KB).
- **Format: unstructured PDFs** → needs PDF parsing (tabular extraction) to get issue size / cut-off
  yield per security. Higher-effort than a JSON API, but the authoritative issuance record.

## Surface 2 — DBIE statistics warehouse (structured time series)
`https://data.rbi.org.in/` (aka DBIE; legacy `dbieold.rbi.org.in`; also **RBIDATA** mobile app).
- Data warehouse of RBI's Dept. of Statistics; **downloadable Excel / CSV / PDF**.
- Seven subject areas; **Financial Market** area carries G-Sec yields — e.g. **10-Year benchmark
  G-Sec yield (computed by FIMMDA)**, month/year-end values, plus rates/spreads series.
- Good for **macro / long-horizon yield history**, NOT per-security valuation (that's FBIL/CCIL).
- Also: Weekly Statistical Supplement (WSS) for outstanding stock; auction calendars.

## Quality
- **Authoritative** (source of record for issuance) but **access is clunky**: issuance data is in
  **PDFs** (parse required); DBIE is a portal (Excel/CSV export, some friction, possible query API
  via the RBIDATA app backend — not yet mapped).
- Per-security *valuation/price* is absent here by design — use FBIL. RBI = issuance + macro yields.

## Fit for the pipeline
- **Pillar: primary issuance (sovereign).** Auction results → new ISINs entering the universe +
  issue size + primary cut-off yield → seeds the universe and the "issued" side.
- Complements: FBIL (daily valuation/price), CCIL (secondary trades), for the full sovereign picture.
  See [[project-indian-bond-research]], [[reference-bond-trade-sources]].
